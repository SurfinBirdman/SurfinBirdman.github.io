This is our story...


It all began in February 2012. An unlikely group was dumped into the middle of doomsday scenario and forced to team up to overthrow an international spy ring. While the spy adventure simulation was cool, Jared and Torie both lamented they were not next door on the 20000 leagues steam punk adventure. At which point they turned each other and said “Wait you know what Steam punk is?!?”


After that revelation and an evening of solving puzzles together, it was just a race to see who would ask the other one out first. Starting with their love of old technology they went to the Museum of Industry in Waltham, where they began to discover all the many things they had in common.


Torie asked Jared how much he liked fire on a scale from 1-10. When Jared answered “a bajillion”, they decided to extend their first date to include a performance from her school’s fire arts club, where she performs with a fire hoop.


From nerdy concerts to late nights of pool, they spent as much time together as possible. But eventually, Torie had to go. She had made plans, before the two of them had met, to travel to BYU for the summer. For their last date they went back to the simulation arcade for their long-awaited steam punk adventure.


They vowed to keep in touch but both agreed they should take a break. Fortunately for this story, their break lasted about as long as it takes to fly from New York to Salt Lake City.


Dozens of Skype calls and thousands of texts later, Jared finished school and flew out to Utah so they could embark on an epic California adventure. From Utah they took a romantic train-ride through the Sierra-Nevadas and on to the Horton Farm in Sacramento.


What started out as a light trip to California, turned into a chance to meet the family. Coincidentally, both extended families from all over ended up intersecting with their path as they meandered down the state, from Sacramento to San Diego. The family Jared still hadn’t met? Torie’s parents.


Back in Boston, Jared went through the arduous process of finding a job and Torie jumped headlong into a full semester. They somehow found time to keep their relationship a priority… and finally meet up with Torie’s Parents.


Their relationship grew and developed as the transient world around them brought new difficulties. They learned how to support each other and how to have fun doing it. Their valentines date consisted of making a cantilever for Torie’s problem set and neither of them can think of a more romantic day.


Through it all they knew there were doing something more than having the best time of their lives, knew they were building something more than a straw and string structure. With sights set on forever, all that remained was actually getting engaged.


With the help of Jared’s mother and sister a plan was hatched. In the next week they built the prop and set the stage (The girls did most of the heavy lifting). Jared would trick her into thinking she was coming for an innocent couple photo-shoot the upcoming weekend.


The stage was set. Torie walked outside and heard a familiar sound coming from around the house. She rounded the corner… and saw a Time-Traveling Police Box! As she approached, the door popped-open. Jared dropped to one knee, and asked her to join him on the greatest adventure of their lives.



And now we’re here… Planning a wedding while Jared works and Torie tries to finish up school. It’s a busy time for both of them, but it’s all worth it.
